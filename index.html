AUTOMATED TRASH EXTRACTOR

This project features the development of a sustainable and innovative solution for removing solid waste from urban canals. Designed and created by students from the Bachelor of Science in Graphics and Design program under the College of Technology at Cebu Technological University â€“ Main Campus, the Automated Trash Extractor reflects a blend of creative design and practical environmental engineering aimed at improving urban waterway cleanliness.

Product

Our product is a solar-powered, semi-automated trash extractor designed to remove solid waste from urban canals efficiently and sustainably. Inspired by the waterwheel mechanism, it minimizes the need for manual labor while promoting eco-friendly waste management. With features focused on safety, energy efficiency, and ease of maintenance, the system offers a practical solution to urban water pollution.

Features
1. Solar Panel used for harnessing sunlight and converting it into electricity or heat, making them an essential tool for sustainable energy. 2. Waterwheel flaps are used to control the flow of water and optimize energy transfer in a waterwheel system. 3. GSM module is used to enable wireless communication between a device and a mobile network. It allows devices to send and receive SMS messages 4. Wiper Motor operates through an electric motor and linkage system, converting electrical energy into mechanical movement to drive the wiper arms back and forth 5. A safety cage in a machine is designed to protect operators from moving parts, flying debris, and hazardous areas. It acts as a physical barrier, preventing accidental contact with dangerous components while ensuring compliance with safety regulations
How does it work?
1. Activation: The user presses the start button, and the machine activates all necessary components. 2. Trash Extraction: The system extracts trash from the canal and transfers it to the internal bin. 3. Bin Monitoring: The machine monitors the bin's fill level and triggers a signal when full. 4. SMS Notification: An SMS is sent to the owner notifying them that the bin is full. 5. Manual Waste Collection: The owner collects and empties the bin, allowing the machine to continue operation.

Frequently Asked Questions

The ATECC is expected to have a lifespan of 3 to 5 years, assuming regular maintenance and proper usage in typical canal conditions. This estimate is based on the durability of the materials selected and the current design's resistance to water exposure and mechanical wear.
The ATECC requires regular cleaning after each use to remove debris from the conveyor and trash bin. Weekly checks should be done to inspect for loose parts, rust, or wear. Moving parts need occasional lubrication, and sensors and wiring must be tested to ensure proper function. If the system uses a motor or battery, power levels should be monitored. Damaged parts should be replaced immediately to maintain efficiency.
Operators must avoid direct contact with moving parts while the machine is running to prevent injury. Always disconnect the power source before performing maintenance or removing debris. Wear protective gloves when handling waste materials collected by the machine. The system should not be operated in extreme weather conditions, such as heavy rain, to prevent electrical hazards. Regular inspection of wiring and components is important to ensure safe operation.
